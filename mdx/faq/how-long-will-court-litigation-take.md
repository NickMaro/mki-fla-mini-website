title: How long will court litigation take?
for: child-custody
order: 4
---

Including time with lawyers, negotiations, correspondence with other lawyers, FDR, and trial, some litigation cases unfortunately drag out over multiple years. This comes down to how flexible or open to change you and your former partner may be.
<br>
<br>
The court may arrange for different types of hearings during this time period, for example case assessments, direction hearings, interim hearings and so on. The aim is to help you reach an agreement before a final decision is made at trial, where as stated above, the court will make orders that are in the best interests of your children, regardless of what you and your partner may believe to be right for your children.
